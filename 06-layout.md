# Layout

## Label logic

The bottle label is divided into two vertical panels.

- **Front panel (left):** the customer-facing, attract-and-inform side. Top to bottom: skin-type icon and name, the large skin-type number watermark behind, the product-style wordmark (FLOW / CORE / DEEP with its stylised letter), a dividing rule, the tagline, the active-ingredient description written to appeal to that skin type, and the horizontal Biotaderm logo at the foot. Centralised, black text unless stated otherwise.
- **Rear / side panel (right):** the skin-type-independent information. Usage and function, the five ethos icons, the full INCI list in strict descending order by mass (including sub-1% ingredients, which is stricter than the legal minimum), website, size, the "Made in Welcombe Devon" organic shape, product life, address, barcode gap, and batch number.

Everything centralised unless stated. The skin-type hue is the loudest element; the style tone stays quiet.

## Web layout grammar

The same restraint carries to the site.

- **Generous negative space.** Let surfaces breathe. It matches the brand's sobriety and gives copy room.
- **Skin-type hue leads, style tone supports.** A skin-type page carries its vivid hue; a shop-by-texture (Flow / Core / Deep) page carries its quiet Tide tone as a wash or accent, never a dominant field.
- **The organic shapes** work as a backdrop or frame, weighted to the relevant skin-type colour, with room around them.
- **Dual navigation.** The site is shoppable two ways: by product line and by skin type. A skin-type finder and per-ingredient pages support this. Keep both paths equally legible; neither is the poor relation.
- **Ingredient transparency is the conversion mechanism.** Published percentages and honest ingredient pages do the persuading, so give them structural prominence rather than burying them below marketing copy.

## Imagery placement

Photography follows the composition rules in `05-photography.md`: product lower or off-centre, generous negative space, the coast leading. Web layouts should be built to sit text in that negative space rather than over a busy hero.
