# Graphics

## The signature device

Biotaderm has one signature graphic device: the organic, interlocking shapes drawn from the logo. They are the brand's visual shorthand and the main decorative element across packaging, web, and social.

## The organic shapes

Soft, rounded forms that sit behind and around the logo, in the five skin-type colours, with the colours multiplying where they overlap. They read two things at once: the algal forms of the coast, and the layered, living quality of skin and microbiome. That dual reading is the point.

Rules:

- Keep the shapes biological and fluid. Never geometric or hard-edged.
- Let the overlap-and-multiply behaviour show. That transparency is part of what makes them feel natural rather than corporate. Do not stack them as opaque blocks.
- Use them as a backdrop, a frame, or a quiet accent, with plenty of room around them. Water and weed, unhurried. Do not crowd them.
- Keep the treatment flat. No drop shadows, no gradients.
- Do not recolour them outside the brand palette.

Because the shapes carry the five skin-type colours, they belong to the skin-type axis, not the style axis. Where they appear alongside a single product, weight them toward that product's hue rather than showing all five equally.
