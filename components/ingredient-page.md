# Component: Ingredient page

The most repeatable surface on the site. One page per ingredient, linked from every INCI entry on every product and label. Because it repeats across the whole catalogue, getting the template right once pays off everywhere, which is why it is the recommended first Claude Design prototype.

## Register

Sober and precise. This is science-and-product content, so the smile stays small. The claims disciplines in `00-principles.md` apply in full and are the whole reason this template is shaped the way it is.

## Structure

1. **Name.** INCI name, with the common name in brackets where there is one. Latin only appears here and in the legal list; the customer-facing description elsewhere uses common names.
2. **Function.** What the ingredient does in the formulation, in plain terms. What role it plays, not what it promises.
3. **Sourcing.** How and where it is obtained. Where an ingredient has a coastal association, this is the place the kinship-not-supply-chain rule bites hardest. For the algal extracts: the same Atlantic species we know from our coast, sourced through specialist French extractors. Never harvested-from-our-shores.
4. **What the research shows.** Split by confidence, and always framed as reviewing published science, never as Biotaderm's own findings.
   - *High confidence:* the well-established, defensible claims (composition, fatty-acid profile, tocopherol content, and the like).
   - *Moderate confidence:* what published research credits the ingredient with, hedged honestly ("studies suggest", "the research credits").
5. **Use with caution.** The honest counterweight. Where market claims are overstated, where a pre-clinical result should not be dressed as a clinical one, where an ingredient carries a sensitisation note. This section is what makes the page trustworthy.
6. **Microbiome relevance.** Direct or indirect, and honest about which. "Pre-clinical" and "indirect" are allowed words.
7. **Cross-links.** Back to the products that contain it, and to the Science hub.

## Non-negotiables on this template

- "The research shows / studies suggest", never "our research / we've proven".
- No before-and-after or condition-change claims. Support, not control.
- Name the caution honestly. A page that only flatters the ingredient reads as marketing and undoes the transparency the brand trades on.
- Watch the known overclaim traps: pomegranate seed oil is not the peel (different polyphenols); chlorella and sea buckthorn microbiome benefits are pre-clinical only; vetiver is a low-to-moderate sensitiser with elevated risk on a compromised barrier; argan's comedogenic rating is disputed, so never "non-comedogenic"; trace citric acid is a pH adjuster, not an active exfoliant; sodium hydroxide is fully neutralised before it reaches skin.

## Worked fragment

A short example of the register, using bladderwrack to show the coastal-kinship handling:

> **Fucus vesiculosus (Bladderwrack) Extract**
>
> *Function.* A marine botanical extract used for its polysaccharide and micronutrient content.
>
> *Sourcing.* Fucus vesiculosus is the same Atlantic species that grows along the north Devon coast we know at low tide. The extract Biotaderm uses is produced by specialist French growers who work with that species. The coast is the kinship, not the supply line.
>
> *What the research shows.* The evidence credits brown-algal extracts of this type with antioxidant activity and with supporting the skin's moisture barrier. Read as a review of the published literature, not as a Biotaderm study.
>
> *Use with caution.* Marine-extract claims are widely overstated in the category. Keep to the compositional and barrier-support evidence rather than dramatic outcome claims.

The visual shell for this page follows the site grammar in `06-layout.md`: generous negative space, ink-ramp headings, a single restrained accent, and the organic shapes only as a quiet frame if used at all.
